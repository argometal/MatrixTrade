## 1. Identity

ArgusForge is a **vision** and a **coordinating system** for preserving, organizing, and transferring **human formation**.

It does not exist only to store information.

Its purpose is to keep usable:

- knowledge;
- experience;
- decisions;
- errors;
- methods;
- context;
- evolution;
- uncertainty;
- relationships;
- documented criteria.

ArgusForge aims for what a person has learned to remain understandable, usable, and transferable by other people or future systems.

---

## 2. Central problem

Most human formation is lost or degraded.

What usually remains:

- documents without context;
- fragmented conversations;
- isolated files;
- incomplete projects;
- decisions without explanation;
- undocumented errors;
- practical knowledge that is hard to reconstruct.

The problem is not only storing data.

The problem is preserving:

- what was learned;
- how it was learned;
- why something was decided;
- what turned out wrong;
- what remained open;
- how each element related to the rest;
- how to transmit it efficiently.

---

## 3. Mission

Preserve and transfer contextualized knowledge, documented judgment, and accumulated formation so they can be used by people, families, organizations, or future intelligences.

ArgusForge does **not** promise to preserve a complete consciousness.

It does **not** replace the person.

It does **not** turn an AI into a moral authority.

It preserves only what was expressed, recorded, validated, or inferred **with traceability**.

---

## 4. Formation principle

Humans grow through education, experience, and transmission.

Technology evolves quickly, but the need to learn, remember, interpret, and transmit knowledge remains.

ArgusForge starts from this principle:

> Accumulated formation must not disappear with time, a platform, an account, a generation, or a tool.

The system must let a future person understand not only the result, but also the process that produced it.

---

## 5. Central ecosystem principle

```text
Products own data.
Memory owns identity.
Argus relates.
MTA observes time.
Chaos captures.
Vault prepares formation.
ArgusForge coordinates.
```

Official interpretation:

| Component | Responsibility |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Products** | Keep their operational data |
| **Memory** | Identity and consolidated knowledge |
| **Argus** | Relationships |
| **MTA** | Change, repetition, and temporal evolution |
| **Chaos** | Raw capture |
| **Vault** | Context and formation for a recipient |
| **ArgusForge** | Coordinates the whole |

No component may absorb every other component’s responsibilities.

---

## 6. ArgusForge

ArgusForge is:

- the coordinating product;
- the common interface;
- the layer that connects engines;
- the environment where a task is declared;
- where formation transfer is prepared;
- the system that can audit which context was used.

ArgusForge is **not**:

- a universal database;
- a single engine;
- an AI model;
- a ChatGPT replacement;
- a simple notes app;
- a copy of Alexandria;
- a direct extension of MatrixTrade.

---

## 7. Chaos

Chaos is the system’s raw entry.

It may receive: text, conversations, images, files, links, repositories, audio, errors, results, observations, ideas, and material acquired via connectors.

Chaos keeps material without requiring prior organization.

A Chaos item:

- is not validated knowledge;
- is not a sealed decision;
- is not a confirmed relation;
- is not a Training Pack;
- is not Memory.

Priority: avoid loss and reduce capture friction.

---

## 8. Memory

Memory represents what the system knows in consolidated form.

It keeps: identity, concepts, current decisions, preferences, stabilized knowledge, relevant states, provenance, versions.

Memory must **not** become the operational store for all products.

Each product keeps its own data.

Memory keeps what is necessary for continuity and transfer.

---

## 9. Argus

Argus answers:

> *What is related, and why?*

It works with: entities, relations, evidence, similarity, contradiction, derivation, dependency, context.

Argus alone does **not** determine: frequency, temporal priority, recurrence, agenda, evolution.

Those belong to MTA.

---

## 10. MTA

MTA answers:

> *What changes, repeats, or gains importance over time?*

It observes: recurrence, evolution, priority, review, trends, repeated errors, currency, temporal sequence.

MTA does not replace Argus.

It does not create general semantic relations.

---

## 11. Vault

Vault prepares **specific formation** for a task or recipient.

It selects and organizes: active context, current decisions, constraints, evidence, known errors, open questions, success criteria.

Vault may produce:

- a Training Pack for an AI;
- an explanation for a person;
- a learning path;
- a project dossier;
- a continuity guide;
- a family transfer package;
- context for execution.

Vault is **not** Memory.

Vault does **not** keep everything.

Vault prepares what is needed for a concrete task.

Detail contract: [`vault-training-layer-contract.md`](vault-training-layer-contract.md).

---

## 12. Formation transfer

ArgusForge does not necessarily deliver the same information to everyone.

Formation must adapt by: recipient, goal, knowledge level, context, risk, available time, required format.

The same base may produce: a simple explanation, a technical document, a study sequence, AI context, a project-continuation guide, or a historical reconstruction.

---

## 13. Relation to AI

AI is a cognitive engine used by ArgusForge.

ArgusForge controls: what context it receives; which decisions are current; what material to exclude; which task to run; which format to produce; which result to keep; which corrections were needed.

AI does not automatically inherit the entire system’s knowledge.

Vault prepares the exact context.

ArgusForge does not aim to create a superior AI by itself.

It aims for an AI that is better contextualized, more consistent, and more useful for a concrete task.

---

## 14. Human judgment

ArgusForge does not replace: responsibility, moral judgment, final decision, human validation, critical interpretation.

The system may preserve documented criteria, but must not automatically turn them into truth.

It must distinguish: fact, evidence, hypothesis, preference, decision, error, contradiction, uncertainty.

Consistency is not the same as correctness.

---

## 15. Personal and family scope

ArgusForge can be useful even if it is never commercialized.

Its first valid purpose is to serve: its creator, family, projects, formation, intellectual legacy, continuity of unfinished work.

A future person should be able to ask:

- What did they learn?
- Why did they decide this?
- What mistakes did they make?
- What did they consider important?
- What remained open?
- How do I continue this work?
- What should I learn first?
- What is fact and what is hypothesis?

---

## 16. Possible use by others

Others may implement ArgusForge if they share similar pains: lost context, forgotten decisions, tool fragmentation, hard knowledge transfer, need for traceability, long projects, heavy use of multiple AIs.

It is not assumed to be a mass product.

Others are not required to adopt all internal complexity.

The visible experience must stay simple.

---

## 17. Simplicity principle

Architecture may be complex.

Interaction must not be.

The user should be able to:

```text
Capture
  → declare a task
  → receive correct context
  → work
  → keep useful learning
```

Engines stay underneath.

The interface must not require understanding the whole architecture to get value.

---

## 18. Provenance principle

Transferred formation must retain, when relevant: origin, date, author, status, currency, confidence, relation to prior decisions, known contradictions.

Nothing must be presented as validated when it is not.

---

## 19. Evolution principle

ArgusForge must allow knowledge to: change, be corrected, be replaced, keep versions, keep history, without destroying provenance.

A new decision must not silently erase the previous path.

---

## 20. Independence principle

ArgusForge must not mandatorily depend on: a single AI, a single vendor, a single app, a single database, Alexandria, MatrixTrade, an external platform, or a specific UI.

Engines and products may connect through clear boundaries.

---

## 21. Alexandria

Alexandria remains frozen as an independent product.

ArgusForge does not depend on Alexandria.

Alexandria does not block ArgusForge.

Future integration only after specific study.

Binding: [`alexandria-frozen-contract.md`](alexandria-frozen-contract.md).

---

## 22. Success criteria

ArgusForge is useful if it verifiably reduces: knowledge loss, repeated explanations, manual context reconstruction, contradictions from forgotten decisions, AI training time, difficulty transferring formation to another person.

It should also improve: continuity, understanding, traceability, learning capacity, intergenerational preservation.

If the system requires more work than it saves, it must be simplified.

---

## 23. First implementation

The first implementation must prioritize:

1. ArgusForge as shell.
2. Chaos as fast entry.
3. Task as goal declaration.
4. Vault as visible context preparation.
5. Human review before using an AI.
6. Measurement of corrections and time saved.

Argus, Memory, and MTA may evolve later.

They must not be invented prematurely.

---

## 24. Final rule

ArgusForge exists so human formation is not reduced to isolated files, lost conversations, or incomplete memory.

It must preserve: what was learned; its context; its evolution; its errors; its relationships; its future usefulness.

It must allow that knowledge to be recovered, understood, and transferred efficiently.
